## Familiar scenario

A Turkish automotive tier-1 exporting to Europe. The supplier ships DP600 coils with a mill certificate attached. Incoming QA stamps "conforms to standard" and the line opens. Three months later, a return from Germany: cracking at B-pillar stamping initiation, fractography showing shear fracture between martensite islands.

Back to the certificate: Mn+Si at the upper bound, CE +0.05 above grade center, Ti/N non-stoichiometric — meaning the Ti added for grain refinement has formed TiN/TiC clusters instead. This is not visible *as a number* on the certificate. Only through interpretation.

## How the analysis works

**1. Chemistry → phase forecast**
C, Mn, Si, P, S, Nb, Ti, V, N, B, Al are combined into CE (IIW), Pcm, Ceq (AWS), Mn/S, Ti/N, and total microalloy sum. From chemistry alone, likely CCT shifts and the expected phase spectrum are estimated.

**2. Mechanical values → consistency check**
Rp0.2 / Rm ratio, A% elongation, Charpy, DWTT %SA for relevant grades. Chemistry–mechanical inconsistency flags. Any value outside the expected band for that grade is questioned.

**3. Microstructural risk forecast**
Inclusion morphology risk (Mn/S, Ca-treatment trace, Al-killed?), segregation band expectation by rolling position, grain size estimate. SEM-EDS recommendation when warranted.

**4. Supplier red-flag scorecard**
Drift trend across your historical heats from the same supplier — plotted. 0–100 score covering chemical drift, mechanical variance, out-of-spec frequency, secondary metallurgy signals.

## FAQ

**Is this a testing laboratory?**
No. No testing is performed — existing certificates and any customer test reports are interpreted metallurgically. Accredited lab tests (SEM-EDS, DWTT, metallographic sectioning) are recommended when warranted.

**Are my certificates safe?**
Files are stored in AES-256 encrypted object storage. Auto-deleted after 90 days. KYK/KVKK disclosure and NDA are accepted at panel entry.

**Turnaround?**
Standard package: 5 business days. Expedited (premium): 24–48 hours. The clock does not start if data is missing.

**Does the report replace an official test certificate?**
No. The report is a metallurgical assessment and risk analysis; acceptance/rejection is the user's decision. Liability limitation is detailed in the Terms of Service.
